Vectors

What are Vectors?

Vectors are points on a graph, they can be in multiple dimentions, from 2D to beyond 3D. From vectors we can calculate direction, magnitude, scalars, and even angles.

How to calculate Vectors?

Simple calculations of vectors are done by simply following the values presented, for example in a 2D vector we follow the first value (X axis position), then we follow the second value (Y axis position) and when we get to the poition we mark the area with a dot. We repeat this for all other points. Vectors can also have a pre-asigned to them as shown below.

Vector multiplication using scalars

We can also multiply two vectors together to get the dot product (scalar output) or to get the cross product (vector output). $$Dot \: Product = a \cdot b = a_x \times b_x + a_y \times b_y$$ $$Cross \: Product = a \times b = (a_x \times b_y) - (a_y \times b_x)$$ From the cross product we can aso find the angle theta by doing: $$\theta = \cos^{-1}(\frac{\vert a \cdot b \vert}{\vert a \vert \vert b \vert})$$

Applications of Vectors?

Vectors are used in close to everything, from physics to computer science, to even graphical processes such as games! In general developers use vectors for 3D models and in rare cases some applications, such as games, have vectors used for performing exploits in 2D and 3D space.
