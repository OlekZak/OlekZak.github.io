Data

What is Data?

Data is a collection of facts, numbers, measurements, words, description and even observeations that represent an idea.

Data can be Qualitative (Descriptive) or Quantitative (Numerical). More over, quantitative data can be Discrete (All numbers or descriptions) or Continuous (within a range)

Applications of Data

Data is used in everything from decision making to finding out what color of car is most popular to even what products sell the most. This is also usefull for Probability and Statistics.

Probability and Statistics

What is Probability and Statistics?

Probability and Statistics is the inference of data to come to conclusions, from figuring out how often something occures to the likely outcome of a game.

How to calculate Probability and Statistics?

As we are using collecting data, we can calculate for:

● Mean

● Mode

● Median

● Range

● Ratio of Probability

● Standard Deviation

Mean is calculated by collecting the sum of all the term divided by the number of terms $$\overline{x} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i}{n}$$

Mode in a standard data set is calculated by finding the most commonly occuring numbers. One thing to note is that in grouped datat set is calculated as so:

Median can be found by either ordering the entire data set in order and finding the middle vale, when 2 numbers are the middles number you add them together and divide by 2, or you can use these equasions: $$\mathrm{Med}(X) = X[\frac{n_{odd} + 1}{2}]$$ $$\mathrm{Med}(X) = \frac{X[\frac{n_{even}}{2}]+X[\frac{n_{even}}{2} + 1]}{2}$$

Range can be calculated by taking the highest value and take away the lowest like so: $$\mathrm{Range} = \mathrm{Highest\:Value} - \mathrm{Lowest\:Value}$$

Ratio of Probability is calculated by dividing the amount of times an actioncan occure by the total number of outcomes, like so: $$Probability = \frac{Number\:of\:ways\:it\:can\:happen}{Total\:number\:of\:outcome}$$ When done to all of of the outcomes you can then right them as $$p_1 + p_2 + p_3 + p_4 ... = 1$$ or it can be written in ratio form as $$p_1 : p_2 : p_3 ...$$

Standard Deviation is calculated by first finding the mean, then subtracting the mean from each score and squaring each deviation, from there divide that sum from the number of scores and square root the output. This iswhat the formula looks like: $$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum{(x_i - \mu)^2}}{N}}$$
