Applications of Differentiation

Differentiation is used for more of purposes than computing the gradient of a curve. Differentiation can be used for figuring out the stationary points of a function and help with optimisation problems.

Another use case is in physics where differentiation is used to study the rate of change in many applications such as velocity and acceleration.

Intergration

What is Intergration?

Intergration is a way of calculating the area, volume and/or points of many things, most commonly used in curved graphs.

How to calculate Intergration?

Intergration is the opposite of Differentiation, meaning we do the opposite to a function. The only exception to calculating intergration is that the final term is sometimes +C as we dont always know the true answer of what the constant is.

Here is a video that explains the basics of intergration.

A good thing to note is that there are to types intergrals, these being Definite and Indefinite intergrals. The difference between them is that definite intergrals have actual points and indefintie intergrals dont have actual points.
