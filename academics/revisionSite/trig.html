Trigonometry

What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is the idea of being able to calculate angles and sides of any non-curved shape, specificaly triangles.

How to calculate Trigonometry?

when dealing with Right-Angled triangles, we can use these equasions: $$Pythagoras' \: Theorem \rightarrow a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$ $$SOH \rightarrow \sin(\theta) \times Hypotenuse = Opposite$$ $$CAH \rightarrow \cos(\theta) \times Adjacent = Hypotenuse$$ $$TOA \rightarrow \tan(\theta) \times Opposite = Adjacent$$ $$$$

When dealing with Oblique triangles, we use these formulas: $$Cosine \: Law \rightarrow c = \sqrt{a^2+b^2-(2ab \cdot \cos(\theta))}$$ $$\rightarrow \cos(\theta) = \frac{b^2+c^2-a^2}{2bc}$$ $$Sine \: Law \rightarrow \frac{\sin(\theta_a)}{a} = \frac{\sin(\theta_b)}{b} = \frac{\sin(\theta_c)}{c}$$ $$\rightarrow \frac{a}{\sin(\theta_a)} = \frac{b}{\sin(\theta_b)} = \frac{c}{\sin(\theta_c)}$$

A good thing to keep in mind is that some questions may be asked in Radians, you should be able to convert between angles and radians with ease. $$Radians \: to \: Degrees \rightarrow x\mathrm{rad} \times \frac{180}{\pi}$$ $$Degrees \: to \: Radians \rightarrow x\mathrm{rad} = x\mathrm{deg} \times \frac{\pi}{180}$$

Applications of Trigonometry

Trigonometry is used in alot of subject areas, from finding the are of shapes, to enabling us to calculate vectors allowing for navigation, construction, engeniering and even 3D graphics for games!
